## TUESDAY, JUNE 17 — Quick Tip

**Caption:**

Quick tip for anyone just starting to can 👇

The #1 mistake beginners make is skipping the rim wipe.

You fill your jar, you're proud of yourself, you slap the lid on — and then it doesn't seal.

Why? Because there's one tiny drop of jam on the rim and it broke the seal. ALL that work. One drop.

Here's what you do:
→ Fill the jar (leave ¼ inch headspace at the top)
→ Grab a clean cloth dampened with hot water
→ Run it around the rim before you put the lid on
→ THEN put the lid on, finger tight

That's it. That is the whole secret. A clean rim = a sealed jar.

I learned this the hard way so you don't have to. That's literally why I started RootedReadyLife — because I made every mistake first and now I can just hand you the shortcut.

Save this post. You'll thank yourself on canning day. 📌

## SUNDAY, JUNE 22 — Sabbath Reflection

**Caption:**

On Sundays I try not to sell anything.

No CTAs. No links. No "click here."

Just this:

There's something in the act of preserving food that feels like hope.

You pick something at its peak — the most it's ever going to be — and you seal it up so it doesn't have to be gone. So you can open it in January when everything outside is gray and cold and taste June again.

I think about that a lot.

We do that with people too, when we're paying attention. We catch someone at their best moment and we hold onto it. We tell the story later. We pass it down.

My abuela grew things I couldn't name and cooked things I still can't replicate and knew things I'm still trying to learn. She's gone now, but I can still taste her kitchen.

That's preservation too.

Rest today if you can. You're doing more than you know. 🌿
